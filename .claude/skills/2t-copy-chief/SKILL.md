## 2. 2T audience snapshot

Primary buyer: **Black American and broader U.S. hip-hop / street-luxury jewelry buyers.** Male-primary, rising female + gift buyer. Fluent in the category — knows plated vs solid, knows the karat/diamond-tester talk, names moissanite directly, spots a fake from a mile out (site, slang, or stone). One core tension:

> "I want it to hit." vs "Don't finesse me — don't sell me one thing as another."

Two equal paths, never custom-only: **SHOP** (catalog = desire) and **CUSTOM** (the dream + margin). Trust drives action. Text/DM removes friction. Social-first — this buyer arrives from TikTok/IG and decides fast.

### Language doctrine (read before writing a single line)

Write in the buyer's **category-authentic vocabulary — never imitation dialect.** The voice is a real Pittsburgh street-luxury jeweler who knows this buyer, not an outsider performing a culture.

- **Do:** speak the shine / identity / process vocabulary the buyer already uses (§5). Short, social-first, confident, direct, product-fluent.
- **Do NOT:** write AAVE grammar, forced dialect, or parody "street" voice. No "drip so crazy," "bussin," "gang," "for the culture" — they read as costume and get roasted (this buyer roasts corny brands). Authenticity comes from knowing the product and the buyer, not from mimicking how anyone talks.
- **The test:** would a real jeweler who serves this buyer every day say it out loud, plainly? If it sounds like a brand *trying* to sound Black, cut it. Confidence + product fluency carry the street weight — not slang density.

**Fears (answer these fast, in their words):** Is the store real? · Are the stones what they say? · Solid or plated? · Will they answer? · Will grillz fit? · Will my photo/logo come out clean? · What will it cost? · Is this site fake/generic?

Top fear = **deception** + **silence**. Every page should defuse both within one scroll.

## 4. Voice rules

1. Lead with the answer to a fear, in buyer vocabulary. Density + specificity over volume.
2. Show proof, never claim it. "Details confirmed per piece" beats "guaranteed."
3. No hierarchy across gold / silver / diamond / moissanite — four equal directions (§9).
4. Direct > slang. Street tone through plain confidence, not imitation.
5. Text-first always. WhatsApp with per-context `?text=` prefill (§11).
6. Name Pittsburgh + real store + "25 years" + "come through" — the moat online sellers can't copy.
7. Every claim gated by verification; default to the safe fallback until proven (§8).
8. Match interface-design rhythm: short Anton lines, one gold `<em>` accent per cluster, arrow `→` on CTAs.

**Rhythm:** short, punchy, one idea per line. `Bring the photo. Bring the logo. Bring the name.`
**Hard no:** N-word, parody AAVE, fake rapper imitation, corporate, boutique, cheap.

## 7. Claim-safety quick gate

`CLAIM_SAFETY.md` is the binding authority. Quick gate before publishing any claim:

- **Verified at correct scope?** → publish prominently (it's a commercial asset). Scope = product / collection / policy. Never use a product-level claim as a sitewide blanket.
- **Not verified?** → use a safe fallback. Never invent.

**Forbidden unless verified at correct scope:** solid gold · real/natural/lab-grown diamond · VVS/VS/SI · GIA · GRA · 14K/10K/18K karat · 925 sterling silver (per-piece badge) · factory-set · authenticated/Rolex · free shipping · lifetime warranty · in stock · production timeline / turnaround · deposit terms · CAD/render/free mockup · grillz fit guarantee / FDA / dentist-approved / mold kit · any review / rating / UGC / follower count / celebrity proof / customer count.

**Business-level confirmed (homepage/category direction only, per-piece badge still gated):** "925 Sterling Silver" · "D-color VVS Moissanite" · "25 years" · address 332 Fifth Ave · @2tjewelers.

**Safe fallbacks:** "Details confirmed per piece." · "Material and stone details confirmed per piece." · "Ask before you buy." · "Text 2T." · "Ask what's in stock now." · "Ask what's running this week." · "Promo terms shown on eligible pieces only." · "No deposit. No rush. We quote first." · "Lease-to-own options may be available through Acima."

**Never:** CZ in any 2T context. Plated described as "gold" without the plating qualifier.

## 8. Material / stone rules — parallel directions, no hierarchy

Gold, silver, diamond, moissanite = four equal directions. Buyer picks by preference, not by better/worse.

**Moissanite:** a major and rising buyer direction — treat it confidently as its own shine, on equal footing. (Directional, not a proven "primary stone" claim.) Name it directly. Never call it a diamond, alternative, substitute, simulant, or fake. GIA grades diamonds, not moissanite — D-color/VVS for moissanite refers to moissanite-specific grading, per-piece gated.
**Silver:** primary direction, never described through price vs gold.
**Diamond:** "diamond" alone reads as natural; label "natural" or "lab-grown" explicitly when known (both per-piece gated). Never shorten "lab-grown diamond" to "diamond."
**Gold:** karat-qualified when stated; plated must say plated.

**Approved framing (safe now):** "Gold · Silver · Diamonds · Moissanite" · "Pick the piece. Then pick the shine." · "Gold or silver. Diamond or moissanite. Different shine. Different direction." · "Details confirmed per piece."

## 11. Promo state machine

The promo strip is the only homepage ticker. Default to State A. Flip only on verified, business-owner-confirmed offer (terms + scope + expiry). Never a standing percentage/BOGO without confirmation (the "20% OFF" line was removed for exactly this).

- **State A — placeholder (default):** "ASK WHAT'S RUNNING NOW · CUSTOM PIECES BUILT HERE · DETAILS CONFIRMED PER PIECE" + "Promo terms shown on eligible pieces only." WhatsApp prefill: "What's running this week?"
- **State B — category signal:** "THIS WEEK AT 2T · [CATEGORY] — ASK WHAT'S IN STOCK · TEXT 2T". Use when a real category focus exists but no priced offer.
- **State C — verified offer:** "[PRODUCT/OFFER] — [MATERIAL] — TEXT 2T NOW · [terms/while available]". Requires confirmed product, terms, dates, exclusions. Material/stone claims per §7–8.

Flip trigger: business-owner confirmation only. No agent-invented promos.

## 12. SocialTeaser / social caption rules

- **Real links/assets only.** No fake posts, captions, views, likes, followers, thumbnails, or competitor screenshots. Handle = **@2tjewelers** in all visible copy.
- TikTok-first (highest-converting format for this buyer); build/behind-the-scenes + close-up shine. But only reference media that actually exists — don't write "watch the build" over an empty placeholder.

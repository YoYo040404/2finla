# 2T Jewelers — Copy Swipe Map

**Purpose:** Strategic map of competitor patterns, social language, and customer language that 2T can adapt safely. Not a copy file. All patterns are summarized and adapted for 2T's voice and claim policy.  
**Source:** VOC research (May 2026) — public pages, social-indexed content, Reddit/YouTube, search results.

---

## 1. Competitor / Source List by Category

### Major Hip-Hop / Street-Luxury Ecommerce
GLD, Frost NYC, TraxNYC, King Ice, Icebox, Johnny Dang, Avianne, Aporro, Moses NYC, ItsHot, Helloice, IceCartel, The Gold Gods, Gold Presidents

**What they do well:**
- Short, direct product labels (style, width, length, weight, material)
- Aggressive promo architecture (BOGO, weekly deals, percentage-off, free shipping thresholds)
- Custom photo/logo upload flows with clear file requirements
- Trust signals: store age, location, customer count
- "In stock" and "ships today" urgency CTAs
- Chain + pendant pairing recommendations
- Diamond watch sections with separate trust copy

**What they do poorly:**
- Many overclaim ("VVS diamonds" without cert, "solid gold" on plated items)
- Generic black/gold templates that look like each other
- Fake scarcity / countdown timers
- Invented celebrity/artist association
- Aggressive store-wide blanket claims that confuse buyers
- Heavy upsell popups that feel predatory

---

### Custom Pendant / Photo Pendant / Logo Pendant Sellers
Gold Presidents, BigHead Custom, Custom GLD, CustomMade, smaller discovered sellers

**What they do well:**
- Clear upload instructions ("send a JPG or PNG, minimum 300 DPI")
- "Send us your logo / photo / sketch" framing
- Before/after custom results
- Simple step-by-step: idea → review → quote → build
- Clear "no deposit until you approve" or "50% deposit to start production" policies
- Chain pairing recommendations

**What they do poorly:**
- Overclaiming timelines ("ready in 3–5 days")
- Fake reviews and fake Instagram UGC
- Hiding stone/material quality behind "premium" language
- Tiny, hard-to-read disclaimers for plating/CZ

---

### Grillz Specialists
ASJ Grillz, Luxe Grillz, Custom Gold Grillz, Gold Teeth God, DrGrillz, GumboGrillz, Gabby Elan, Dolly Cohen, Bling Cartel, IceCube Grillz

**What they do well:**
- Fit process explained clearly: mold kit instructions, impression tray, return shipping
- Step-by-step production walkthrough
- Gold karat and stone type stated clearly per piece
- "Text us before ordering" and "ask about timing" prompts
- Photos of real grillz on real people (not just product shots)

**What they do poorly:**
- Some overclaim ("guaranteed fit" without defined process)
- Some imply dental approval without evidence
- Some use generic countdown timers or fake stock counters

---

### Diamond Watches / High-End Street Jewelry
Pristine Jewelers, Eliantte, Benny Da Jeweler, Icebox watches, TraxNYC watches, Frost NYC watches, Johnny Dang watches

**What they do well:**
- "Inquire about this piece" model for high-ticket items
- Stone type and grade stated per watch
- Condition stated clearly (new / pre-owned / custom modified)
- "Schedule a viewing" or "text for availability" CTAs
- Separate trust language for watch authentication

**What they do poorly:**
- Some imply blanket authenticity without per-piece verification
- Brand name references that may create confusion about product origin
- Vague "natural diamonds" claims without certification shown

## 3. Customer Language (Reddit / YouTube / Search / Forums)

Summarized from public review content, forum posts, YouTube comment sections, and search-indexed Q&A. Not copied verbatim.

### What Buyers Actually Say

**Before buying:**
- "Is [brand] legit?"
- "Are their diamonds real?"
- "Is this solid gold or plated?"
- "How long does custom take?"
- "Do they actually respond?"
- "Has anyone gotten grillz from them? How's the fit?"
- "Will the photo come out clean on a pendant?"
- "How much does a custom logo pendant actually cost?"

**After buying — positive signals:**
- "It hits in person."
- "Looks way more expensive than it cost."
- "They were responsive."
- "The chain is heavier than expected."
- "Grillz fit perfectly after the mold."
- "My name pendant came out clean."

**After buying — negative signals:**
- "Looks cheap up close."
- "Not as shiny as in the photos."
- "They said diamonds — it was CZ."
- "Took way longer than they said."
- "Never responded to my DM."
- "Grillz didn't fit — no recourse."
- "Couldn't tell it was plated until it started fading."

### What This Means for 2T Copy

- Never say "diamond" when you mean CZ or moissanite unless clarified
- Never say "solid gold" without per-piece verification
- Never promise timelines without current confirmation
- Always include "text before you buy" and "details confirmed per piece"
- Always have a responsive text/WhatsApp channel — and reference it in copy
- Show the back of pendants, chain thickness, stone setting — in media
- For grillz: explain the fit process clearly so buyers feel safe before ordering

## 5. Promo Mechanics Map

### What Competitors Use (patterns only — adapt safely)

- BOGO on chains (buy one length, get one shorter free — with terms)
- Percentage-off sitewide or by category (10%, 15%, 20% with exclusions)
- Weekly featured piece or "Special of the Week" module
- Limited in-stock drops with "X pieces left" or "ask before it moves"
- Free shipping threshold (e.g., orders over $X)
- Flash sale countdown (do not fake scarcity)
- Custom order specials ("custom pendant this week, ask for pricing")
- Bundle deals (chain + pendant, watch + bracelet)

### What 2T Can Build (when verified)

- Special of the Week module on homepage and category pages
- Percentage-off banner with dates, exclusions, eligible categories
- BOGO on chains or pendant sets with written terms
- Free shipping on eligible orders above a threshold
- Featured drop module with real in-stock product details
- "Ask what's running now" placeholder until promo is confirmed

### Safe Default Until Verified

> Ask what's running this week.
> Text 2T to ask about current deals.
> Promo terms shown on eligible pieces only.

## 9. What 2T Must Not Copy

- Generic black/gold luxury template look
- Soft boutique jewelry tone
- Blanket lifetime warranty claims without policy
- Blanket free shipping without policy
- Fake VVS / natural diamond claims without per-piece verification
- Fake scarcity (countdown timers, "only 2 left" without real inventory)
- Celebrity / influencer association without evidence
- Over-polished luxury phrases
- Imitation AAVE / parody street slang
- Hiding material details (plated labeled as gold, CZ labeled as diamond)
- Calling moissanite a "diamond alternative," "diamond substitute," or "diamond simulant" — moissanite is its own stone and a legitimate 2T product; it must be named clearly and never positioned as inferior
- Framing silver as inferior to gold or as a budget option — silver is a primary 2T material direction
- "Moissanite diamond" or "VVS moissanite diamonds" — moissanite is not a diamond; do not conflate the two
- Identical card grids that look AI-generated
- Invented UGC or fake customer photos
- Fake reviews

## 10. Material & Stone Language Map

*(Added 2026-05-28 — based on 21-source market research pass)*

### The Rule

2T sells gold, silver, diamonds, and moissanite as parallel directions. No hierarchy. No downgrade framing.

### What to Use

| Context | Approved copy |
|---|---|
| Category or homepage mention | "Gold or silver. Diamond or moissanite." |
| Positioning separator | "Different shine. Different direction." |
| Stone/material fallback | "Material and stone details confirmed per piece." |
| Custom flow close | "Ask what fits the piece." |
| Moissanite standalone | "Moissanite — its own stone." |
| Silver standalone | "925 sterling silver." *(when confirmed per piece)* |

### What to Avoid

| Phrase | Why avoid |
|---|---|
| "diamond alternative" | Positions moissanite as runner-up |
| "diamond substitute" | Same — implies inferiority |
| "diamond simulant" | Legal/GIA term with negative consumer connotation |
| "moissanite diamond" | Moissanite is not a diamond — FTC risk |
| "fake diamond" | Self-defeating; never write this |
| "looks like a diamond" | FTC deception-by-implication risk |
| "affordable alternative to diamond" | Re-introduces hierarchy through price |
| "cheap silver" / "cheaper than gold" | Downgrade framing — forbidden |
| "budget option" | Forbidden for any 2T material or stone |

### Claim Verification Required Before Publishing

These claims must be confirmed per piece before use:
- "925 sterling silver"
- "D-color moissanite" / "VVS moissanite"
- "GRA-certified moissanite"
- "GIA-graded diamond"
- "Lab-grown diamond" / "Natural diamond"
- "14K / 10K / 18K gold"
- "Gold-plated over 925 sterling silver"
- "Solid gold"

Safe default until confirmed: "Material and stone details confirmed per piece."

### CZ Note

CZ is NOT a 2T product. Do not include CZ in any 2T copy, category, or selector context.
